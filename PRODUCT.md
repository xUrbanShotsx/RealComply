# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Two co-equal visitors: **principals and licensees-in-charge** (the buyers — they own the compliance problem, approve spend, need confidence that the business is protected) and **operations / compliance staff** (the champions — they will use the software daily, manage deadlines, run audits). Both must leave the page feeling this product was built for them specifically, not sold to them generically.

Context: a busy real estate office environment — property management, sales, and administration all running simultaneously. Compliance is a constant background pressure, not a once-a-year exercise.

## Product Purpose

Real Comply is a compliance management platform built specifically for Australian real estate businesses. It consolidates everything: policies and procedures management, CPD/licence tracking and renewal reminders, audit readiness documentation, trust accounting compliance, and AML compliance per property transaction. The platform replaces spreadsheets, email reminders, and paper trails with a single source of truth. Success means a licensee-in-charge sleeps easier and an auditor finds zero surprises.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Modern · Confident

Voice is authoritative but not cold — the tone of a senior compliance lawyer who speaks plainly. No jargon-for-jargon's-sake. No softening language. Statements, not suggestions. Australian in cadence.

Emotional goal: confidence. Visitors should leave feeling that compliance is a solved problem, not an ongoing anxiety.

## Anti-references

- Rex, Console, PropertyMe, MRI — legacy real estate software aesthetics (dated UI, low-contrast, data-heavy without clarity)
- Generic SaaS cream/beige + blue button template
- Government portal or regulatory-body website vibes
- Overly corporate/enterprise cold aesthetics

## Design Principles

1. **Authority through clarity.** Compliance is high-stakes. Every element should communicate that this is a serious, professional tool — not by being intimidating, but by being unambiguously clear.
2. **Specificity earns trust.** Generic claims don't reassure compliance professionals. Name the Australian legislation, the exact pain point, the specific workflow. Vagueness reads as ignorance.
3. **Show the product.** Real estate principals are busy and skeptical. The landing page must demonstrate the product's clarity — if the page itself is hard to scan, they won't believe the product is easy to use.
4. **Confident scale.** This is a business-critical tool, not a nice-to-have app. Design at the scale of what's at stake — not timid cards and muted grays.
5. **Local, not local-washing.** Australian real estate compliance has specific legislative frameworks (the Property and Stock Agents Act, state-specific trust accounting rules, AUSTRAC obligations). The product knows this. The page should too — without costuming it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast vs background. Large text ≥3:1. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Reduced-motion media query respected on all animations.
